1. What are cookies and similar technologies?

Cookies are small text files stored by your browser when you visit a website. They are used to remember information about your visit or preferences. By "similar technologies" we mean, among others: localStorage, sessionStorage, IndexedDB, web beacons, pixels, and device fingerprinting techniques.

Pursuant to Article 11.7a of the Dutch Telecommunications Act ("cookie law") and Article 7 GDPR, prior consent is required for the placement or reading of non-necessary cookies. For strictly necessary (functional) cookies and cookies that serve solely to measure visitor numbers under strict conditions, no consent is required.

6. Stripe Radar (payment security)

On pages where payment functionality is offered (such as buying a subscription, paying an invoice), the Stripe.js library is loaded from the domains js.stripe.com, m.stripe.com, q.stripe.com and r.stripe.com. This library is necessary to securely process your payment.

Stripe places cookies and uses similar technologies on the Stripe domains in this context (not on beebeeb.io). Stripe uses these technologies for, among other things:

  • Secure payment functionality (Stripe Elements / Stripe Checkout)
  • Fraud prevention via Stripe Radar (device fingerprinting, behavioural analysis, session analysis)
  • Compliance with legal obligations including anti-money laundering legislation and sanctions screening

For these technologies Stripe acts as an independent controller (see section 4 of our Privacy Policy). For more information we refer you to Stripe's privacy notice and cookie policy.

7. Consent, withdrawing and modifying

On your first visit to beebeeb.io we request via a cookie banner your consent for optional cookies (such as Matomo with cookies, where applicable). Strictly necessary cookies and security cookies are placed without consent because they are necessary for delivering the Service.

You can adjust or withdraw your cookie preferences at any time via:

  • The "Cookie settings" link in the footer of our website
  • Your browser's cookie and site data settings

Withdrawing consent has no retroactive effect — data collected before withdrawal remains part of the existing dataset but will be deleted in accordance with the retention periods.

8. Managing cookies via your browser

In addition to our cookie settings, you can always manage cookies via your browser. Most browsers allow you to block, delete or receive notifications before a cookie is placed. Please note that disabling strictly necessary cookies may result in the Service not working (fully).

General instructions:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions
